üöó Getting Around: The Transportation Reality Check

The Uber/Lyft Surge Nightmare
Let's be real: Uber surges HARD on King Street between 10 PM - 2 AM, especially on Friday and Saturday nights. We're talking $40+ for a 10-minute ride. Don't be that group scrambling for rides at midnight when prices are 3x normal.
üí° Pro Move: Schedule pickups at Marion Square (corner of Meeting & Calhoun) or Charleston Place Hotel (205 Meeting Street) instead of directly on King Street. Walk 2 blocks, save $20-30 per ride. These spots have better pickup logistics and less congestion.
Party Bus/Limo: Worth It For Groups
For groups of 8+, booking a party bus or limo for the whole night (4-6 hours) runs $400-800 total. That's $50-100 per person for unlimited transportation, no surge pricing, and no waiting. Plus you can drink between stops (legally, as long as the driver doesn't).
üéâ Local Companies: Charleston Black Cab, Absolutely Charleston, and Charleston Party Ride offer great group transportation. Book 2-3 weeks ahead for weekends.
Walking It? Know Your Distances
Charleston's downtown is walkable, but those cobblestones add up. Here's the real walk times:
  • Lower King to Upper King: 20-25 minutes (farther than it looks on the map)
  • King Street to Waterfront: 10-15 minutes depending on where you start
  • Market to King Street bars: 5-10 minutes
‚ö†Ô∏è Reality Check: Those "quick 10-minute walks" become 20+ minutes when you're in heels, slightly tipsy, and stopping for photos. Factor in extra time.
Pedicabs: Tourist Fun or Legit Option?
Pedicabs are everywhere downtown, especially near Market Street. They're $5-10 per person for short trips, but negotiate the price BEFORE getting in. Great for: short hops when you're tired, avoiding surge pricing, and fun photo ops. Not great for: long distances or when you're in a hurry.

Here's what nobody tells you: Charleston's historic cobblestone streets will DESTROY stilettos. Those cute Instagram heels? They're going straight between the stones, and you're going straight to the ground. Even locals who wear heels bring backup flats.
