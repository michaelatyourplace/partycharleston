Why Charleston is Perfect for an All-Ages Bachelorette

Let's face it: planning a bachelorette party when the maid of honor is 24 but the bride's cousin is 19 can be a nightmare. Charleston is the perfect solution. This city's charm isn't hidden behind a 21+ ID check. It's in the world-class food, the stunning scenery, the one-of-a-kind shopping, and the unique group activities.

You can have a full, luxurious, and wildly fun weekend that everyone in the group will enjoy. This guide is your complete playbook. We've built a 3-day itinerary that's packed with fun, plus in-depth guides on every activity.

A Perfect 3-Day All-Ages Bachelorette Itinerary

Here is a customizable, proven itinerary for a flawless weekend. Use our detailed guides (linked below) to plan each step.

Day 1: Arrival, King Street & Creative Fun

  • Afternoon (3 PM): Arrive, check into your Airbnb or hotel. Put on your first coordinated outfit!
  • Late Afternoon (5 PM): Start with a King Street Stroll. This is the perfect all-ages "crawl" for shopping, people-watching, and soaking in the energy.
  • Evening (7 PM): Kick off the party with a perfect ice-breaker. Book a private class at a spot like Candlefish. We've got a full guide to Charleston's best creative workshops.
  • Late Night (9 PM): Skip the bars and go straight for the legendary late-night dessert crawl. A slice of cake at Kaminsky's or gelato at Carmella's is the real late-night party. or a late-night show call Michael at Your Place for details!

Day 2: Water, Pampering & Spooky Fun

  • Morning (10 AM): Get out on the water. A private sailing charter or a dolphin tour is a classic Charleston experience that everyone can enjoy. Check our guide to bachelorette-friendly boat tours that aren't just booze cruises.
  • Afternoon (1 PM): Time to relax. Split the group: some can go shopping, while others enjoy a day of pampering. We show you how to book a luxury group spa day.
  • Evening (7 PM): Get dressed up for a nice group dinner. Charleston is a world-class food city, so make a reservation at an upscale spot like Husk or 82 Queen.
  • Late Night (9 PM): Do something memorable. Instead of a club, do a spooky ghost tour through a graveyard or see a hilarious improv show at Theatre 99.

Day 3: The "Sleep-In Fun" & Departure

  • Morning (11 AM): No 8 AM alarms. Let everyone "sleep in fun" and then head out for the grand finale: the bachelorette brunch. This is a non-negotiable Charleston ritual. Find the perfect, 'gram-worthy spot with our guide to bachelorette-friendly brunch.
  • Afternoon (1 PM): One last group photo, then head to the airport, exhausted but happy.
